Background

Climate change is recognized as a tremendous challenge towards sustainable economic, human and environmental development worldwide. The five Central Asian countries are among the ECA’s region most vulnerable to climate change and building resilience to climate change impacts is a priority for poverty reduction and shared prosperity in Central Asia. Recognizing the importance of climate actions, Central Asian countries acknowledged that climate risks transcend borders between them and emphasized the importance in establishing a regular platform to collaborate in effective and scaled-up resilience. All five countries of Central Asia agreed that a regional program for climate adaptation and mitigation is a priority and can build on, and strengthen existing experience and initiatives in the region. Echoing the request of the Central Asian countries, the World Bank Group in collaboration with national counterparts, developed the Climate Adaptation and Mitigation Program for Aral Sea Basin (CAMP4ASB), which aims to enhance regionally coordinated access to improved climate change knowledge services for key stakeholders (e.g., policy makers, communities, and civil society) in participating Central Asian countries as well as to increased investments and capacity building that, combined, will address climate challenges common to these countries. On November 3, 2015, the World Bank Board of Executive Directors approved an allocation of US$38 million from the International Development Association (IDA) to finance the first phase of the CAMP4ASB regional program. This approved financing includes US$9 million for Tajikistan, US$14 million for Uzbekistan, and US$15 million for regional activities to be implemented by the Executive Committee of the International Fund for Saving the Aral Sea (EC-IFAS) with support from the Regional Environmental Center for Central Asia (CAREC) for day-to-day regional coordination and implementation. CAMP4ASB project is well accommodated within existing and planned regional initiatives on climate change and sustainable development, and addresses the needs, which have been commonly prioritized by the countries of Central Asia, on accessing to climate investments and technologies, new methodologies, capacity building and information exchange.

Project objective

CAMP4ASB aims to enhance regionally coordinated access to improved climate change knowledge services for key stakeholders (e.g., policy makers, communities, and civil society) in participating Central Asian countries as well as to increased investments and capacity building that, combined, will address climate challenges common to these countries. CAMP4ASB is comprised of regional and national components. CAREC is responsible to implement the Component 1, which aims to strengthen the knowledge and capacity base for climate action and facilitate regional dialogue and multi-stakeholder engagement for an effective climate response at scale, and Subcomponent 3.1, which is to oversight, coordination, and implementation support at the regional level.
